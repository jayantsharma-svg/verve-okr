SECTION 1

Overview & Scope

This Privacy Policy ("Policy") describes how Capillary Technologies Pvt. Ltd. ("Capillary," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects personal information in connection with Verve by Capillary ("Verve," "the App," or "the Service") — an enterprise OKR (Objectives and Key Results) tracking application designed exclusively for internal use by companies that are clients of Capillary Technologies.

This Policy applies to all users of Verve, including the mobile application (iOS and Android) and any companion web interface, wherever they are accessed. It does not apply to any other Capillary products, websites, or services unless those materials directly link to this Policy.

B2B Context: Verve is provided to employer organisations ("Employers") as a business-to-business (B2B) service. If you are an employee using Verve, your Employer has engaged Capillary to process your work-related data on its behalf. Please also review your Employer's own internal data-use policies, which may contain additional details specific to your organisation.
SECTION 2

Who We Are

Capillary Technologies Pvt. Ltd. is a technology company headquartered in Bangalore, India, with offices across Asia, the Middle East, and the United States. We develop enterprise SaaS solutions including loyalty management, customer engagement, and — through Verve — employee performance and goal tracking.

For the purpose of data-protection law, Capillary acts as a data processor on behalf of your Employer, who is the data controller for the personal data you provide while using Verve in a professional capacity. Where Capillary independently determines the purposes and means of processing (for example, to operate and improve the platform), Capillary acts as a data controller in its own right.

SECTION 3

Data We Collect

We collect only the minimum data necessary to provide and support the Verve service. The categories below describe every type of personal information we may collect.

3.1 Account & Identity Data

When you sign in to Verve — either through your company's Google Workspace (Google SSO) or with an email address and password — we collect:

  • Full name
  • Work email address
  • Profile photo (if provided by your Google Workspace account)
  • Organisational role or job title (if supplied by your Employer during provisioning)
  • Department or team assignment (if supplied by your Employer)

3.2 OKR & Goal Data

The core purpose of Verve is to help you and your team set, track, and achieve Objectives and Key Results. We store:

  • Objectives you create, edit, or are assigned to
  • Key Results linked to those objectives, including descriptions, metric targets, and ownership
  • Alignment relationships between objectives (e.g., individual objectives aligned to team or company objectives)
  • Timestamps of creation and last modification

3.3 Progress & Check-In Data

  • Check-in updates you submit (progress notes, confidence ratings, numerical progress values)
  • Date and time of each check-in
  • Comments or notes attached to check-ins

3.4 Authentication & Technical Data

  • Authentication tokens (OAuth 2.0 tokens for Google SSO; hashed passwords for email/password login — we never store passwords in plaintext)
  • Device type and operating system version (for technical support and compatibility purposes)
  • App version number
  • Basic server logs: IP address, request timestamps, HTTP status codes (retained for a limited period for security monitoring and troubleshooting)

3.5 Data We Do Not Collect

No advertising data. No tracking. No data selling. Verve does not contain advertising SDKs, third-party analytics trackers, or behavioural profiling tools. We do not collect precise geolocation, financial information, health data, biometric data, or any sensitive personal information beyond what is listed above.

SECTION 4

How We Use Your Data

We use the data described in Section 3 strictly for the following purposes:

4.1 Providing the Service

  • Authenticating your identity and maintaining secure sessions
  • Displaying your OKRs, your team's OKRs, and company-level goal hierarchies
  • Recording and presenting check-in updates and progress history
  • Sending in-app notifications or email reminders about upcoming check-ins or deadline milestones (where enabled by you or your Employer)

4.2 Service Operation & Improvement

  • Diagnosing technical issues and providing customer support
  • Monitoring system health, availability, and security
  • Improving product features and user experience based on aggregated, de-identified usage patterns (no individual profiling)

4.3 Reporting to Employers

As part of our B2B service, Capillary may provide your Employer with aggregated dashboards, OKR completion reports, and data exports (e.g., to Google Sheets or CSV) that contain your work performance data. This is a core feature of the service and is done under the authority and direction of your Employer.

4.4 Legal & Compliance Obligations

  • Complying with applicable laws, regulations, and lawful requests from public authorities
  • Establishing, exercising, or defending legal claims
No automated decision-making: Verve does not make automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects about you without human oversight. OKR scores and completion percentages are purely informational tools for you and your manager.

SECTION 6

Data Sharing & Third Parties

We do not sell your personal data. We do not rent, sell, or trade your personal information to any third party for marketing, advertising, or any commercial purpose unrelated to providing the Verve service.

6.1 Your Employer

Your Employer, as the data controller, has access to the OKR and check-in data you create within their Verve workspace. This is the intended and primary purpose of the application. Administrators and managers within your organisation may view your goals and progress as configured by your Employer.

6.2 Infrastructure & Cloud Service Providers

Verve's backend is hosted on Google Cloud Platform (Cloud Run, Cloud SQL, and related Google Cloud services). Google acts as a sub-processor under Capillary's direction and is contractually prohibited from using your data for its own purposes. Google Cloud's data processing commitments are covered under Google's Data Processing Addendum.

6.3 Authentication Provider

If you sign in using Google SSO, you authenticate directly with Google via the OAuth 2.0 protocol. We receive only the identity token (name, email, profile photo) that your Google Workspace administrator has authorised. We do not receive access to your Google Drive, Gmail, Calendar, or any other Google services beyond this limited identity scope.

6.4 No Third-Party Advertising or Analytics

Verve contains no third-party advertising SDKs, no behavioural tracking pixels, and no social-media integration. We do not share personal data with data brokers, advertising networks, or any analytics service that would profile you as an individual.

6.5 Legal Disclosures

We may disclose personal data when required by law, court order, or legitimate governmental authority, or where necessary to protect the rights, property, or safety of Capillary, our clients, or others. Where legally permissible, we will notify the relevant Employer before making such a disclosure.

6.6 Corporate Transactions

In the event of a merger, acquisition, asset sale, or restructuring involving Capillary Technologies, your data may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify affected Employers in advance, and any successor entity will be bound by terms no less protective than this Policy.

SECTION 7

Storage & Security

7.1 Infrastructure

All Verve data is stored and processed on Google Cloud Platform infrastructure located in data centres that meet ISO 27001, SOC 2, and PCI DSS standards. Google Cloud data centres operate in geographically redundant configurations to ensure availability and resilience.

7.2 Encryption

  • In transit: All communications between the Verve app (mobile and web) and our servers are encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher. We enforce HTTPS for all endpoints; unencrypted HTTP connections are rejected.
  • At rest: All data stored in our databases and cloud storage buckets is encrypted at rest using AES-256 encryption, managed by Google Cloud Key Management Service (Cloud KMS).
  • Passwords: If you use email/password authentication, your password is hashed using a strong, salted one-way hashing algorithm (bcrypt). We never store or transmit passwords in plaintext.

7.3 Access Controls

  • Access to production systems is restricted to authorised Capillary engineering personnel on a need-to-know basis.
  • All administrative access requires multi-factor authentication (MFA).
  • Role-based access controls (RBAC) within the application ensure that employees can only see the data scoped to their team or organisation as configured by their Employer's administrator.
  • All internal access to personal data is logged and auditable.

7.4 Security Monitoring

We maintain continuous security monitoring, automated vulnerability scanning, and regular security reviews. In the event of a personal data breach that poses a risk to individuals, we will notify affected Employers without undue delay and in accordance with applicable law.

Responsible disclosure: If you discover a security vulnerability in Verve, please report it responsibly to privacy@capillarytech.com. We appreciate and take all security reports seriously.
SECTION 8

Data Retention

We retain personal data for as long as necessary to provide the Verve service and fulfil the purposes outlined in this Policy, unless a longer retention period is required by law.

Data Category Retention Period
Account & profile data For the duration of the Employer's active subscription, plus up to 90 days after account closure to allow for data export requests
OKR & check-in data For the duration of the Employer's active subscription, plus up to 90 days after account closure
Server & access logs Up to 90 days for routine logs; up to 12 months for security incident logs
Backup copies Up to 30 days for automated backups; deleted in accordance with the above schedule thereafter

When an Employer's subscription ends or is terminated, we will delete or anonymise all associated personal data in accordance with the terms of the service agreement, subject to any legal holds or obligations to retain certain records.

Individual employees may request deletion of their personal data by following the process described in Section 9.

SECTION 9

Your Rights

Depending on where you are located and applicable law, you may have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

Right Description
Access Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
Rectification Ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data. Note that most profile data is managed by your Employer's administrator.
Erasure / Deletion Request that we delete your personal data, subject to legal retention requirements and your Employer's policies.
Data Portability Request your OKR and check-in data in a structured, machine-readable format (e.g., CSV or JSON).
Restriction Ask us to restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances.
Objection Object to processing based on legitimate interests where your particular situation warrants it.
Withdraw Consent Where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time without affecting prior processing.

How to Exercise Your Rights

Because Verve is an enterprise application, some rights (particularly access, rectification, and erasure) are primarily exercisable through your Employer, who controls your account and organisational data. We recommend first contacting your HR department or IT administrator.

For requests that must be directed to Capillary (for example, where your Employer is no longer accessible or has ceased operations), please contact us at:

  • Email: privacy@capillarytech.com
  • Subject line: "Data Subject Request – Verve"
  • Include: your full name, work email address, Employer name, and a clear description of your request

We will respond to verified requests within 30 days. In complex cases, we may extend this period by up to an additional 60 days and will inform you accordingly.

Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe your data-protection rights have been violated, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant supervisory authority in your jurisdiction. For individuals in the European Economic Area or United Kingdom, this is your national data protection authority. For individuals in India, this is the Data Protection Board of India (once constituted under the DPDP Act, 2023).
